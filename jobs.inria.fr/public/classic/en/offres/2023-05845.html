2023-05845 - PhD Position F/M Control of a sailing boat: from virtual to real, and back

Contract type : Fixed-term contract

Level of qualifications required : Graduate degree or equivalent

Fonction : PhD Position

Level of experience : Up to 3 years

About the research centre or Inria department

The Inria Lille - Nord Europe Research Centre was founded in 2008 and employs a staff of 360, including 300 scientists working in sixteen research teams. Recognised for its outstanding contribution to the socio-economic development of the Hauts-De-France région, the Inria Lille - Nord Europe Research Centre undertakes research in the field of computer science in collaboration with a range of academic, institutional and industrial partners.

 The strategy of the Centre is to develop an internationally renowned centre of excellence with a significant impact on the City of Lille and its surrounding area. It works to achieve this by pursuing a range of ambitious research projects in such fields of computer science as the intelligence of data and adaptive software systems. Building on the synergies between research and industry, Inria is a major contributor to skills and technology transfer in the field of computer science.

Context

As part of an existing collaboration between Scool and Prof. Esteban Clu, UFF Brazil, we are working on
the application of reinforcement learning to the control of a sailing boat. We work on a digital twin of
a sailing boat and on a real sailing boat.

A simulation of a rather generic sailing boat has been made, reaching a certain level of realism.
Likewise, early works have made reinforcement learning able to control this simulation.


The job environment is made of Scool, a research group having a strong expertise on reinforcement
learning, and Prof. Clua who has designed and implemented a real autonomous sailing boat.

Assignment

The Ph.D. student will continue this work, following the line of work already performed.
This work will be continued in close relation with Philippe Preux in Scool, and Esteban Clua in Brazil.
We expect that a publication may be submitted as an outcome of this contract. We also expect that
this work will be continued further in the future by other researchers.
