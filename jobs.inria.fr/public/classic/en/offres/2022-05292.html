2022-05292 - Computer Vision internship: weakly supervised computer vision

Contract type : Fixed-term contract

Level of qualifications required : Master's or equivalent

Fonction : Temporary scientific engineer

Level of experience : Recently graduated

Context

The intern will be working on Inria Paris site located in the very heart of Paris, next to the main Gare de Lyon train station. There will have important interaction with the computer vision group and the intern is expected to participate to regular group reading, seminars, etc.

Assignment

We are looking for a research internship (6 months) in the Astra-Vision group, ASTRA team, Inria Paris.  The group is currently expanding and has some exciting subjects coming up. In the context of an internal study on computer vision algorithms we require a computer vision intern.

The internship aims at bridging the use of reducing our dependency to abundant datasets and costly labeling process. In particular, we seek to understand the changes of scene understanding with computer vision in adverse conditions such as low light environment or adverse weather conditions. The research work will rely on existing deep vision architectures and the intern will have to review the relevant literature and propose new research directions. 

The candidate is expected to have great knowledge of computer vision and deep learning theory, as well as some practical projects / internships experience on the matter. Good coding ability and experience of deep frameworks are prerequisites.

The internship will be in the Astra-Vision group which research deals with scene understanding with a focus on adverse weather and self-supervised learning.

He will be co-supervised by Raoul de Charette (Inria)

Main activities

The precise outline of the work will be refined with the candidate.

Skills

Required skills:

  • Great knowledge of Computer Vision and Deep Learning
  • Knowledge of the main deep vision architectures
  • Ability to read and analyse a scientific article
  • Experience of some of the main deep frameworks
  • Great coding ability
  • Prior scientific experience is a plus
  • The intern must be fluent in english

Please also ensure that current diplomacy policies allow your venue on the french territory given the current special sanitary conditions.

About Inria

Inria is the French national research institute dedicated to digital science and technology. It employs 2,600 people. Its 200 agile project teams, generally run jointly with academic partners, include more than 3,500 scientists and engineers working to meet the challenges of digital technology, often at the interface with other disciplines. The Institute also employs numerous talents in over forty different professions. 900 research support staff contribute to the preparation and development of scientific and entrepreneurial projects that have a worldwide impact.
