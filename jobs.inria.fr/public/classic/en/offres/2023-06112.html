2023-06112 - PhD Position F/M DOCT2023-Premedical Causal inference for combining experimental and observational data

Contract type : Fixed-term contract

Level of qualifications required : Graduate degree or equivalent

About the research centre or Inria department

The Inria centre at Université Côte d'Azur includes 37 research teams and 8 support services. The centre's staff (about 500 people) is made up of scientists of diﬀerent nationalities, engineers, technicians and administrative staff. The teams are mainly located on the university campuses of Sophia Antipolis and Nice as well as Montpellier, in close collaboration with research and higher education laboratories and establishments (Université Côte d'Azur, CNRS, INRAE, INSERM ...), but also with the regiona economic players.

With a presence in the fields of computational neuroscience and biology, data science and modeling, software engineering and certification, as well as collaborative robotics, the Inria Centre at Université Côte d'Azur  is a major player in terms of scientific excellence through its results and collaborations at both European and international levels.

Assignment

Assignments :
With the help of Julie Josse, the recruited person will be taken to conduct innovative research on transportability in causal  inference.

For a better knowledge of the proposed research subject :
A state of the art, bibliography and scientific references are available at the following URL, do not hesitate to log in Premedical website: PhD Thesis Proposal.

Collaboration :
The recruited person will be in connection with Erwan Scornet Assistant Professor at Ecole Polyechnique.

Responsibilities :
The person recruited is responsible for developping methods of data integration in the context of causal inference  and will take initiatives for deriving statistical properties of generalization estimators for Risk Ratio in particular as well as suggesting ways to handle missing values

Main activities

Main activities (5 maximum) :

  • Developping transportability estimators for binary outcome and studying their statististical properties (asymptotic and finite sample) in particular with different subsets of variables
  • Developping methods to handle sporadic or systematic missing values: establishing identifiability or developping sensitivity analyses
  • Reviewing Meta-Analyses methods for state of the art and suggest improvements and limitations in particular for Risk Ratio
  • Testing the suggested methods using numerical simulations

Additional activities (3 maximum) :

  • Participate to the creation of an R package on the topic

 

Skills

Technical skills and level required : Engeneers schools, Master in Statistics, Machine Learning, Biostatistics, Data-science or related fields. Experience with machine learning and high-dimensional statistics, Strong statistical computing skill. 

Languages : English, French

Relational skills : Excellent writing and communication skills

Other valued appreciated :  Background on causal inference is a plus as well as experience in R
