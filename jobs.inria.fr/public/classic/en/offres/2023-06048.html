Assignment

Low-Power Wide Area Networks (LPWANs) allow battery-powered end-devices to communicate over long distances (up to 10-30 km) with very low power consumption. LPWANs are used in Internet of Things (IoT) applications for smart city such as pollution monitoring, smart metering, and asset tracking. Most deployed LPWANs use Long-Range Wide Area Network (LoRaWAN), which relies on the Long-Range technology (https://www.semtech.com/lora). Recently, LoRaWAN has introduced the Long-Range Frequency-Hopping Spread Spectrum (LR-FHSS) modulation [1] to enable the communication of a massive number of end-devices and achieve better network capacity. While these characteristics opened the door for new applications such as the communication of end-devices situated on the ground with low Earth orbit (LEO) satellites, LR-FHSS is also the perfect solution for dense network deployments in city environments, being especially important for applications where scalability is crucial [2, 3].
The goal of this thesis is to study the performance of LR-FHSS in LoRaWAN terrestrial networks, and more particularly in a smart city scenario. The thesis includes the following topics:

  • The experimental study of LR-FHSS communication links in terms of range and robustness. This study will take into account different data-rates of the LR-FHSS modulation.
  • The experimental study of the coexistence of LR-FHSS and LoRa. While the two modulations are largely different, they typically share the same frequency channels. Considering that both technologies will co-exist in the foreseeable future, it is of uttermost importance to make such a study.
  • The improvement of LR-FHSS communications and its integration with the LoRaWAN protocol stack.

[1] LoRa Alliance Technical Committee Regional Parameters Workgroup. LoraWAN regional parameters. Technical Report RP002-1.0.3, LoRa Alliance, 5 2021.
[2] G. Boquet, P. Tuset-Peiro, F. Adelantado, T. Watteyne, and X. Vilajosana. LR-FHSS: Overview and performance analysis. IEEE Communications Magazine, 59(3):30–36, 2021.
[3] M. A. Ullah, K. Mikhaylov, and H. Alves. Analysis and simulation of LoRaWAN LR-FHSS for direct-to-satellite scenario. IEEE Wireless Communications Letters, 11(3):548–552, 2022.

Main activities

To study this topic, the following method will be followed:

  • The candidate will study the state of the art on the recent LR-FHSS modulation.
  • The candidate will explore the performance of LR-FHSS achievable with real hardware in indoor and outdoor environments.
  • The candidate will explore how how LoRa and LR-FHSS interfere with each other, depending on a series of physical layer parameters.
  • The candidate will develop new algorithms in order to better integrate the LR-FHSS modulation with the LoRaWAN stack in a smart city scenario.
