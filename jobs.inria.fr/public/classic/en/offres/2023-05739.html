Context

Funded by the Inria Challenge (Defi Inria) FedMalin involving 10 Inria research teams, the position will be hosted by the WIDE  team at Inria at the University Rennes, in close collaboration with the SPIRALS team at Inria Lille.

The candidate will work in the WIDE team in Rennes, and will collaborate closely with other involved teams, as well as international researchers.  It is therefore expected that the candidate will regularly travel to visit our partner teams. All travel expenses will be covered by Inria. 

 

Assignment

The main objective of this position consists in developing a framework to test decentralized machine learning algorithms in a cluster environment, in a production environment, and in a combination of the two. The framework should enable developers to test algorithms on a testing environment and then seamlessly deploy them into a production setting.

The recruited engineer will start by studying in detail existing libraries for federated learning and decentralized learning. In particular, they will start by studying two existing libraries.

Flower federated-learning library developed by the University of Cambridge and the German Company Adap.
DecLearn a library developed by the Magnet team at Inria at the University of Lille

Based on this analysis we will decide whether to build a stand-alone library or integrate our efforts into the Flower project.

The engineer will then design and implement the necessary components to build a library supporting decentralized machine-learning algorithms and applications.

A critical part of the work will consist in testing the library and writing documentation in the form of both API documentation and tutorials for implementing simple decentralized models.

After implementing and testing the library, the engineer will help researchers implement the algorithms resulting from their research work and will contribute to deploying experiments on large-scale clusters and on real mobile devices.

Main activities

- Understanding the existing implementations of libraries for federated learning.
- Designing and implementing the necessary components for buidling a library for decentralized machine-learning
- Testing
- Writing documentation
- Helping researchers implement their own algorithms
- Deploying, running experiments, and collecting results.
