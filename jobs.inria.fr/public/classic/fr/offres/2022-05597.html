Contexte et atouts du poste

In the context of the ERC STG MAJORIS European project, our aim in this internship, is to investigate recent tools from information geometry to derive optimization algorithms that can reach global minimizer of non-convex functions while preserving reasonable complexity.

 

Subject: Optimization plays a key role in many scientific fields. Although most methods require knowledge about the gradients, sub-gradients, or Hessians of the objective function to be minimized, this function often comes in the form of a black-box. This means that it is only possible to evaluate the objective function point-wise, without any knowledge about the function's analytical expression. Such problems, which are often non-convex, arise for instance when the objective is computed using complex simulations, as in hyper-parameter tuning or inverse problems in physics.

Efficient methods for these problems need to avoid local minima and saddle points in order to find the global minimum of the objective function. Sampling plays a key role in such algorithms. In particular, many schemes, such as the cross-entropy method, sample points from parametric probability distribution that is iteratively adapted. These procedures use sampling to identify the best regions of the search space and steer the subsequent probability distributions in this direction.

Many procedures based on parametric distributions can be understood through the lens of information geometry, which arises when one measures distances using the Kullback-Leibler divergence. This geometry has already led to many insights, unifying many statistical procedures as in our recent work [1], or many global optimization schemes as it is done with the IGO framework of [2]. However, this is not the only geometry one can consider. 

In this internship, we specifically plan to explore recent works in the field of optimal transport and variational inference, with the aim to build new global optimization methods with sounded convergence and practical efficiency.

[1] T. Guilmeau, E. Chouzenoux, V. Elvira. Regularized Rényi divergence minimization through Bregman proximal gradient algorithms. https://arxiv.org/abs/2211.04776


[2]  Y. Ollivier and L. Arnold and A. Auger and N. Hansen, Information-Geometric Optimization Algorithms: A Unifying Picture via Invariance Principles, Journal of Machine Learning Research, vol. 18, no. 18, pp. 1-65, 2017.

 

Mission confiée

Missions : The recruited student will investigate new optimization schemes for global optimization. The main challenges are : algorithm design, convergence analysis, practical implementation on small to medium scale non-convex problems.


Environment : The intern will be supervised by Emilie Chouzenoux (Inria Saclay, PI of the ERC
project MAJORIS). He/she will be working in closed collaboration with Thomas Guilmeau, PhD student of OPIS. He/she will also interact with Victor Elvira (School of Mathematics, University of Edinburgh). The intern student will join the Inria Saclay team OPIS (https://opis-inria.eu/). He/she will be located in the Centre de la Vision Numérique, in CentraleSupélec campus, Saclay, France. He/she will enjoy an international and creative environment where research seminars and reading groups take place very often. Informatic material expenses will be covered within the limits of the scale in force.

Organization : The proposed offer is dedicated to end-of-study internship of Master 2 students. Students looking for a "stage de césure" are also encouraged to apply. The starting/end dates are flexible, with a minimum duration of 5 months.

A propos d'Inria

Inria est l’institut national de recherche dédié aux sciences et technologies du numérique. Il emploie 2600 personnes. Ses 200 équipes-projets agiles, en général communes avec des partenaires académiques, impliquent plus de 3500 scientifiques pour relever les défis du numérique, souvent à l’interface d’autres disciplines. L’institut fait appel à de nombreux talents dans plus d’une quarantaine de métiers différents. 900 personnels d’appui à la recherche et à l’innovation contribuent à faire émerger et grandir des projets scientifiques ou entrepreneuriaux qui impactent le monde. Inria travaille avec de nombreuses entreprises et a accompagné la création de plus de 180 start-up. L'institut s'eﬀorce ainsi de répondre aux enjeux de la transformation numérique de la science, de la société et de l'économie.
