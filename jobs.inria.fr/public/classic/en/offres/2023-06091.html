Assignment

Assignments :

Quantum topology is an area of low dimensional topology that studies topological objects, such as knots and 3-manifolds, with tools from mathematical physics.

The computational complexity of topological invariants coming from quantum topology has proved extremely rich and deep. One very noticeable result is that approximating additively the Jones polynomial of a knot, evaluated at the 5th root of unity, is BQP-complete. This constitutes one of the rare natural BQP-complete problems, and allows us to exploit the topology of knots as further structure to quantum computing.

This thesis is about refining and generalizing our understanding of the quantum complexity of topological quantum invariants of knots and 3-manifolds.

  • One line of research will be the classification of the complexity (BQP-membership and BQP-hardness) of ``colored'' invariants (such as the Witten-Reshetikhin-Turaev invariants of links issued from Lie algebras, and Turaev-Viro invariants of 3-manifolds) evaluated on the complex plane. Colored invariants give wide infinite families of topological invariants, with distinct classical computational complexity ; hence a non-trivial classification of quantum complexity is expected.

  • A second and parallel line of research will be the refinement of the approximation guarantees for computation of quantum invariants on a quantum computer. Currently, computation with polynomial size quantum circuit guarantee additive approximation of quantum invariants to within a factor exponential in some parameter on the input, which is of both topological and combinatorial nature. This offers avenues for improvement by refining the dependency of the approximation with finer and smaller topological properties of the input.

  • A third line of research will be the classification of classical complexity (#P-hardness) of these quantum invariants (their exact computation and/or precise approximation) via quantum reductions (PostBQP-hardness).
  • Finally, a last line of research will consists of studying the special structure of knots and 3-manifolds when simulating quantum computation with BQP-hard problems. This will require notably the development of software and experimental plateforms in quantum computing for and by topology.
