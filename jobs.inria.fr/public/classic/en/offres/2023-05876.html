Keywords— Deep Learning, Mathematical Morphology, Computer Vision, Binarization

Context: Deep Neural Networks have taken the world by storm in the last 10 years, with unprecedented performance in many tasks related to computer vision. However, current models require many memory and computing resources. One way forward is to explore binary neural networks, where the weights are purely binary, in the hope of reducing memory and computation considerably. However Binary Neural Networks can be hard to train.

The goal of our research project is to develop and improve upon binary morphological neural networks (BiMoNNs) [1, 2], a type of neural network that uses mathematical morphology to enable exact binarization of network weights and activations. The main objective of this project is to explore the potential of BiMoNNs for various computer vision tasks, such as image classification, object detection, and semantic segmentation, and to evaluate their performance compared to other state-of-the-art methods. BiMoNNs have shown promising results, both theoretical and experimental. However, there is still much to be explored and improved upon in this field, particularly in terms of optimizing the binarization process and extending the concept to other domains
