2023-06150 - PhD Position F/M Domain decomposition methods for aeroacoutic models (IDP 2023)

Contract type : Fixed-term contract

Level of qualifications required : Graduate degree or equivalent

Other valued qualifications : Master in applied Mathematics

Fonction : PhD Position

Assignment

  • Helioseismology is the scientific field that aims to determine the nature of the interior of the sun through the measurements at the surface of the star. These studies involve the introduction of linear aeroacoustic models that aim to reduce the complexity of nonlinear fluid mechanics models such as Euler or Navier-Stokes models. Situations where the fluid can be described as a small amplitude perturbation of a mean flow lead to drastic reduction of the computational costs while keeping accurate simulations. In this thesis, we will study two generalizations of the linearized Euler model: the Galbrun and Goldstein-Visser systems.

    We have identified two major issues that block their use in numerical modeling
     1. Is it possible to couple these aeroacoustic models in the framework of a domain decomposition method? What coupling conditions allow to define well-posed and easily discretizable problems?
     2. How can the Galbrun and Goldstein-Visser equations be closed by absorbing boundary conditions that allow for an accurate and cheap description of the outside of the computational domain?

Main activities

The first part of the thesis aims at proposing a domain decomposition method for aeroacoustics. The Trefftz and HDG methods are numerical methods allowing to parameterize the solutions of wave propagation systems with boundary unknowns called numerical fluxes. The Trefftz and HDG methods thus provide a natural framework for domain decomposition for hyperbolic problems. First, a Trefftz or a HDG method will be developed as well as coupling conditions from the theory of Riemann solvers to transfer the information between subdomains. Then, a selection strategy will be defined to choose which model should be used in each subdomain. 

The second part of the thesis will focus on the development of boundary conditions for the Galbrun and Goldstein-Visser models. The aim is to extend the results of a former PhD thesis dedicated to the linearized Euler equations. Tools from microlocal analysis will be used to define high-order absorbing boundary conditions. An analysis of the developed boundary conditions will be conducted in order to prove the well-posedness as well as established error estimates to characterize the accuracy of these approximate conditions.

The resulting numerical methods will be implemented within the HPC Hawen code developed by  Makutu including an iterative resolution of the linear systems of GMRES type.
