Contexte et atouts du poste

 

Mission confiée

 The complementarity of learning systems, between episodic memory that rapidly stores particular cases and semantic memory that slowly extracts generalities, has allowed major advances in computational neuroscience as well as in artificial intelligence (AI). On the one hand, it has allowed better exploration of the consolidation mechanism, whereby the hippocampus replays particular instances stored in episodic memory in order to accelerate and better balance semantic learning in the cortex, which in turn can feed and control the hippocampus more accurately. AI has seized on these principles to improve the performance of machine learning algorithms by coupling associative memories that learn examples by heart and error-directed layered networks that generalize. In particular, this has addressed the problem of continuous learning when one has at one's disposal, not a large corpus representative of a domain, but a sequence of examples whose distribution may evolve over time, which is representative of many real-world situations (Parisi et al., 2019).
Recently, these domains have undergone a major new step, with the use of generative models. On the one hand, it has been shown in neuroscience, that beyond simply returning stored examples, the hippocampus was also able to propose new virtual
examples, built from the combination of examples, thus being able to form the basis of our imaginative function (Kumaran et al., 2016). Remarkably, it was also shown that this construction of imagined examples could be controlled by the semantic memory of the cortex. On the other hand, AI has taken up these principles by developing generative models, often based on diabolo network architecture, in encoder-decoder, whose use of the decoder part fed by random examples or controlled by another semantic source, could effectively generate new examples without having to resort to an example base that is
often expensive to maintain.
Many recent AI success stories rely on these advances. The Transformer families use these encoder-decoder architectures. Larger application domains can be targeted because we have a model of the world without managing a huge corpus. This is particularly true for language and conversational agents based on GPT models. But there are still major conceptual problems to be solved in these approaches, in particular concerning the nature and mechanisms of these generative models. The aim of this thesis is to clarify these aspects, based in particular on computational neuroscience models that exploit the knowledge of neuroscience on the functions and brain circuits involved.

 

 

Principales activités

Various aspects will thus be discussed. First, at the level of anatomy and general architecture of the circuits involved, depending on the authors, this generative model can be described with a major involvement of CA1 in the hippocampus or of the cortex with the participation of its prefrontal and entorhinal regions (not counting mentions of the possible role of the cerebellum). It will therefore be important to study these various possibilities, asking in particular how this part of the hippocampus, whose main function is supposed to be the storage of particular cases, can be brought to extract statistical regularities from examples stored in episodic memory.

Secondly, still at the architectural level, we can also ask questions about the diabolo architecture, which was mainly imposed by the fact that it is known in modeling to allow the realization of models of the world by reducing the noise and extracting latent variables. One may ask whether this architecture is compatible with the corresponding anatomical knowledge and whether the associated mechanisms in the models such as variational encoders to achieve stability in the representations can be justified biologically or whether other ingredients are to be considered, such as the organization of hierarchical information flows (Stoianov et al., 2022) or that may lead to different forms of compositionality (Kurth-Nelson et al., 2023).

Third, this study of generative models could also be an opportunity to propose other major advances in their use. An important interest in presenting a generative model through the extraction of latent variables is that these variables correspond to the most meaningful dimensions of the phenomenon under consideration and that they can therefore contribute to the explicability of this phenomenon, which is another issue on which AI is currently stumbling. It will therefore be interesting to analyze how these generative models could be exploited in this perspective, but also how the questions mentioned above can enrich this analysis. In particular, the location of the generative model in the brain network, rather hippocampal or cortical or, as suggested by other authors, at different locations in the cortex, could indicate different semantic bases to these latent variables and thus to the phenomena they could help explain (Spens and Burgess, 2023).

A propos d'Inria

Inria est l’institut national de recherche dédié aux sciences et technologies du numérique. Il emploie 2600 personnes. Ses 200 équipes-projets agiles, en général communes avec des partenaires académiques, impliquent plus de 3500 scientifiques pour relever les défis du numérique, souvent à l’interface d’autres disciplines. L’institut fait appel à de nombreux talents dans plus d’une quarantaine de métiers différents. 900 personnels d’appui à la recherche et à l’innovation contribuent à faire émerger et grandir des projets scientifiques ou entrepreneuriaux qui impactent le monde. Inria travaille avec de nombreuses entreprises et a accompagné la création de plus de 180 start-up. L'institut s'eﬀorce ainsi de répondre aux enjeux de la transformation numérique de la science, de la société et de l'économie.
