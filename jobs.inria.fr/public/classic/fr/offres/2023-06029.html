Mission confiée

Motivation and biological context

Glioblastoma (GBM) is the most frequent primary malignant tumors of the central nervous system. These infiltrating tumors are associated with an extremely poor prognosis. Immunotherapy by direct stimulation of the immune system with immune checkpoint blockade (ICB), as anti-PD1 and anti-PDL1 therapeutic antibodies, are effective treatments for multiple malignancies but most of GBM patients do not respond to this immunotherapeutic approach.

Several reasons are associated with resistance of GBM to ICB: (i) low immunogenicity -i.e. low mutational burden-, (ii) immunosuppressive microenvironment and, (iii) the blood brain barrier (BBB) limiting drug penetration. Indeed, the BBB is a biophysical and biochemical obstacle for ICB to reach their target. Preclinical and clinical studies showed that ultrasound mediated BBB opening (UMBO) using low-intensity pulsed ultrasound (LIPU) is a safe and effective method to enhance delivery of therapeutic agents to the brain in preclinical and clinical settings. In preclinical models of GBM, efficacy of anti-PDL1 therapeutic antibody is enhanced when administered in combination with LIPU. LIPU increase the delivery of anti-PDL1 antibody to the brain and the tumor, and activate microglia cells. This combination doubled the number of long-term survivors compared to anti-PDL1 administration alone.  In the same line, more recently T cell immunoglobulin domain and mucin domain protein 3 (TIM3) has been shown to be the most common co-inhibitory immune checkpoints in GBM expressed by both tumor and non-tumor cells. In addition, TIM3 has been shown to promote tumor growth.

Therefore, inhibiting TIM3 using anti-TIM3 therapeutic antibody appears as a promising strategy. However, therapeutic antibodies are large molecules that do not cross the BBB spontaneously. Therefore, in the present study, we will evaluate the therapeutic and biologic effects of anti-TIM3 antibodies with and without LIPU-MBO in preclinical mathematical model of GBM to increase their brain bioavailability and anti-tumor activity.

Experimental materials and methods (Data)

Our partners will conduct experiments to collect data, they will use two syngeneic GBM mouse models: (i) GL261 and (ii) NFPP10. GL261 is murine GBM and NFPP10 is genetically engineered mouse GBM. Both cells are transduced to express the luciferase allowing noninvasive monitoring of tumor growth. Tumor cells will be engrafted in the right caudate nucleus-putamen of immunocompetent C57BL/6 mice using a stereotactic frame. In both GBM mouse models, TIM3 expression will be assessed using RT-qPCR and immunostaining. Multiplex immunostaining will be performed to identify cell populations expressing TIM3. Tumor bearing mice will be treated with anti-TIM3 antibody plus LIPU-MBO. Several control groups will be used: (i) untreated tumor bearing mice, (ii) tumor bearing mice treated with anti-TIM3 alone and (iii) tumor bearing mice treated with LIPUMBO alone. Each group will include 12 mice. The preclinical trial will be conduct with both GBM models (i.e. GL261 and NFPP10).

The endpoints will be: (i) toxicity using limit-points, (ii) anti-tumor efficacy in terms of tumor growth, (iii) anti-tumor efficacy in terms of mice survival, (iv) immune microenvironment modulations and, (vi) brain penetration of anti-TIM3.

The immune microenvironment will be assessed using multiplex immunostaining and FACS sorting. Brain concentration of anti-TIM3 will be assessed using mass spectroscopy.

Mathematical modeling

We will develop mathematical models for both GBM mouse models, (i) GL261 and (ii) NFPP10, with and without BHE opening, based on ordinary differential equations (ODE), delay differential equations (DDE) and structured partial differential equations (PDE). The dynamics of GBM and TIM3 will be studied mathematically in the same scenarios as the experiments: (i) untreated tumor-bearing mice, (ii) tumor-bearing mice treated with anti-TIM3 alone, and (iii) tumor-bearing mice treated with LIP-UMBO alone. Once the data are available, we will proceed to estimate the model parameters. Analysis of the stability of equilibria and their bifurcation as a function of model parameters (in the autonomous case). Variation of the frequency and duration of opening of the UMBO and its impact on the efficiency of the treatment. Numerical simulation of solutions in different scenarios.

Expected results

We hypothesize that LIPU-MBO administration will enhance the efficacy of anti-TIM-3 antibodies against in vivo preclinical models of GBM and will be the rational for combining anti-TIM3 with other therapeutic strategies and the rational for a clinical trial in GBM patients. Based on the mathematical model, we will study the best therapeutic strategy combining the use of LIPU-MBO and the administration of anti-TIM-3 antibodies.

Principales activités

The work will follow the following steps:

Bibliographic study, state of the art of cancer modeling and its pharmacokinetic/pharmacodynamic treatment. 

Construction of a mathematical model in the four scenarios: untreated tumor-bearing mice, tumor-bearing mice treated with anti-TIM3 alone, tumor-bearing mice treated with LIP-UMBO alone, and tumor-bearing mice treated with LIP-UMBO and anti-TIM3.  

Model parameter estimation and sensitivity analysis.

Mathematical and numerical analysis of the models.

A propos d'Inria

Inria est l’institut national de recherche dédié aux sciences et technologies du numérique. Il emploie 2600 personnes.
