A propos du centre ou de la direction fonctionnelle

The  Inria University of Lille centre, created in 2008, employs 360 people including 305 scientists in 15 research teams. Recognised for its strong  involvement in the socio-economic development of the Hauts-De-France region, the Inria University of Lille centre pursues a close relationship with large companies and SMEs. By promoting synergies  between researchers and industrialists, Inria participates in the  transfer of skills and expertise in digital technologies and provides  access to the best European and international research for the benefit  of innovation and companies, particularly in the region. For more than 10 years, the Inria University of Lille centre has been located at  the heart of Lille's university and scientific ecosystem, as well as at  the heart of Frenchtech, with a technology showroom based on Avenue de Bretagne in Lille, on the EuraTechnologies site of economic excellence dedicated to information and communication technologies (ICT).

Contexte et atouts du poste

As part of an existing collaboration between Scool and Prof. Esteban Clu, UFF Brazil, we are working on the application of reinforcement learning to the control of a sailing boat. We work on a digital twin of a sailing boat and on a real sailing boat.

A simulation of a rather generic sailing boat has been made, reaching a certain level of realism. Likewise, early works have made reinforcement learning able to control this simulation.

The job environment is made of Scool, a research group having a strong expertise on reinforcement learning, and Prof. Clua who has designed and implemented a real autonomous sailing boat.

 

Mission confiée

The post-doc will continue this work, following the line of work already performed.

This work will be continued in close relation with Philippe Preux in Scool, and Esteban Clua in Brazil.

We expect that a publication may be submitted as an outcome of this contract. We also expect that this work will be continued further in the future by other researchers.

 

Principales activités

Main activities (5 maximum) :

  • further develop the simulator of water and winds and their interaction with a virtual sailing boat
  • further investigate the use of reinforcement learning to control this virtual sailing boat
  • investigate how transfer learning may be used to transfer from silico to real sailing boat
  • investigate how transfer learning may be used to transfer upon a series of different virtual sailing boats.

Additional activities (3 maximum) :

  • report orally on activities
  • write reports

Compétences

Technical skills and level required : gazebo, ocean and wind simulation, sailing boat simulation, deep reinforcement learning.

Languages : English. Portugese is a plus.

Relational skills : should be very good to interact with collaborators in Scool and with Prof. Clua's team in Brazil.

Other valued appreciated : hard dedicated worker.
