About the research centre or Inria department

The Inria research centre in Lyon is the 9th Inria research centre, formally created in January 2022.  It brings together approximately 300 people  in 16 research teams and research support services.

Its staff are distributed at this stage on 2 campuses: in Villeurbanne La Doua (Centre / INSA Lyon / UCBL) on the one hand, and Lyon Gerland  (ENS de Lyon) on the other. The centre's teams work closely with research and higher education institutions (ENS de Lyon, UCBL, INSA Lyon, etc.), their laboratories, and other research organisations in Lyon (CNRS, INRAE, competitiveness clusters, etc.), but also with Lyon and regional economic players. Many international collaborations are also underway.

The Lyon centre is active in the fields of software, distributed and high-performance computing, embedded systems, quantum computing and privacy in the digital world, but also in digital health and computational biology.

Context

Committed to innovation, Inria creates the conditions for profitable encounters between public research, private R&D and companies. Inria transfers its results and skills to startups, SMEs and large groups in fields such as health, transport, energy, communication, security and privacy protection, the intelligent city and the factory of the future. Inria is also developing an entrepreneurial culture that has led to the creation of 200 startups. Inria participates in the Horizon Europe program, which addresses major scientific and socio-economic issues for the Europe of tomorrow.

Assignment

Reporting to the head of the "Transfer, Innovation & Partnerships" department (STIP) of the Inria Lyon center and in collaboration with the CPPI Europe of the Grenoble center, the Post-Award Europe Innovation Projects and Partnerships Officer will have the following tasks:

For the two centers in Lyon and Grenoble:

  • Accompanies the research teams' staff in their partnership and technology transfer initiatives with the aim of increasing the socio-economic impact of European projects.
  • Acts as an interface between researchers, external partners and Inria's internal departments.
  • Is an actor in the local ecosystem and in the Inria Transfer business network in order to enhance the value of project results and detect partnership and transfer opportunities.
  • These activities are carried out in close collaboration with the research teams, the other departments of the Lyon and Grenoble Centers (SAF, SRH, SCM) and Inria's departments, in particular the DGDI and DPE.
  • In coordination with the CPPI Europe Grenoble, provides long-term training in pre-award aspects
  • Assists research team personnel in their partnership and technology transfer initiatives with the aim of increasing the socio-economic impact of research projects.
  • Acts as an interface between researchers, external partners and Inria's internal services.
  • Is an actor in the local ecosystem and in the Inria Transfer business network in order to enhance the value of project results and detect partnership and transfer opportunities.
  • These activities are carried out in close collaboration with the research teams, the other departments of the Lyon Centers (SAF, SRH, SCM) and Inria's Directorates, in particular the DGDI.

 

For the Lyon center ("Non-European" Partnership missions):

  • Acts as an interface between researchers, external partners and Inria's internal departments.

Main activities

  • Development of relationships with academic partners, industrialists, SMEs, and regional innovation structures (clusters, institutions); with a view to setting up European projects or partnerships
  • Detecting, analyzing and supporting business creation and/or technology transfer projects resulting from European projects

The European Partnerships and Innovation Projects Officer has a privileged link with Inria's European Partnerships Department (DPE) and Inria's Innovation Department (DGD-I) and participates in meetings of the Inria national network.

He/she works in close collaboration with the other Innovation Project and Partnership Officers (CPPI) of the department (startups and industrial partnerships) in order to promote the transfer of European projects and the creation of startups.

About Inria

Inria is the French national research institute dedicated to digital science and technology. It employs 2,600 people. Its 200 agile project teams, generally run jointly with academic partners, include more than 3,500 scientists and engineers working to meet the challenges of digital technology, often at the interface with other disciplines. The Institute also employs numerous talents in over forty different professions. 900 research support staff contribute to the preparation and development of scientific and entrepreneurial projects that have a worldwide impact.
