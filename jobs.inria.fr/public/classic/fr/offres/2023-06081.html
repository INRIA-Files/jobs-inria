Mission confiée

Under the supervision of Dr. Juan Fraire and with the help of Dr. Oana Iova and Prof. Hervé Rivano, the recruited postdoc will work on the design, development, simulation, test, and implementation of a new multi-modal adaptive ad-hoc system for the maritime communications.


Maritime Communications
The maritime domain is spread over vast surfaces. We focus on the region marked between the territorial boundary at 12 nautical miles from the coast to the exclusive economic zone (EEZ), which spans up to 200 nautical miles. Links operating in the maritime context are limited in signal strength, reliability, throughput, bandwidth, and latency due to channel sparsity, wave-induced instability, fading effects, evapotranspiration, and Rayleigh scattering.


State-of-the-art RF solutions in the VHF band mostly leverage Digital Selective Calling (DSC) and Automatic Identification System (AIS) systems. The limited bandwidth and range are the main limiting factors in these solutions. Data rates up to 10 kbps over a few tens of km can only ensure half-duplex voice calling or messaging.
On the other hand, extended WiFi technology is limited to a few kilometers of the coast (e.g., the MareFi project). Cellular network access and broadband solutions based on WiMAX or LTE are restricted to near-shore locations, although in some cases can cover up to 100 km from the shore. Alternative solutions include collaborative peer-to-peer (P2P), e-LORAN, and underwater acoustic communications (e.g., Sound Surveillance System or SOSUS), but these still need to be improved.


The use of High-Altitude Platforms (HAPs), Unmanned Aerial Vehicles (UAVs), and Low-Earth Orbit (LEO) satellites as relay nodes are also appealing to bridge the long-range in maritime links. However, satellites are both bandwidth- and cost-constrained. Geostationary satellites also impose latency challenges.

As a result, the overall maritime communication network is highly challenging regarding bandwidth and latency and partitioned topologies that demand adequate low-power, long-range, delay- and disruption-tolerant technologies yet to be developed.


Objective
This postdoc position aims to investigate adaptive ad-hoc communication network solutions to overcome the limiting effects of maritime communications. The final goal is to allow maritime applications in a ground control station to communicate telemetry, data, voice, images, and telematic inputs to and from vessels. Use cases include sonde measurements, vessel tracking, offshore wind parks, and maritime oil platforms.
The strategy to overcome the aforementioned state-of-the-art limitation is the integration of multiple communication technologies in the maritime device. This objective will be accomplished in two levels: Low-Power Wide Area (LPWAN) links and Delay Tolerant Networks (DTN).


1) LPWAN Link: The candidate will work in the design, simulation, development, and implementation of a maritime device capable of switching between device-to-ground, device-to-device, and device-to-relay (LEO satellite, HAP, or UAV) links. The candidate technology is LoRaWAN, standardized by the LoRa Alliance.

2) DTN Network: An overlay delay and disruption-tolerant network protocol spanning the heterogeneous maritime links will be identified, simulated, developed, and integrated into the device. The candidate technology is the Bundle Protocol (BP), standardized by the IETF and CCSDS.

Principales activités

  1. Survey scientific and technical literature about a) maritime communications and networking, b) LPWANs for the IoT and its applications in device-to-device and device-to-relay (LEO satellite, HAP, or UAV), and c) DTN for the maritime environment.
  2. Identify the best LPWAN and DTN technologies considering LoRaWAN and BP as baseline candidates. Define the optimal operational parameters for concrete use case scenarios which shall also be defined.
  3. Design an integration approach for DTN and LPWAN using Convergence Layer Adapters (CLAs) to convey and route DTN data via LPWAN links. Study suitable compression and fragmentation strategies (e.g., inspired by Static Context Header Compression and Fragmentation or SCHC). Propose suitable supporting algorithms for managing, routing, securing, and orchestrating the maritime device and network.
  4. Implement a simulation toolchain to evaluate the resulting LPWAN/DTN maritime protocol stack. Execute extensive simulation campaigns to determine the economic and energy cost versus data throughput trade-off while fine-tuning the protocol stack parameters.
  5. Implement a device prototype and a suitable test bench where the resulting algorithmic framework and protocol stack could be embedded and evaluated. Perform laboratory and field tests and trials leveraging the prototype. Adequate collaboration partners will be identified to this end.
  6. Contribute to the corresponding standardization and certification process in relevant institutions (e.g., IETF, CCSDS, LoRa Alliance, etc.).

Compétences

Applicants with Ph.D. in Computer Sciences, Engineering, or related domains are welcome. Mathematical background, performance evaluation, wireless networking, and practical skills with programming languages (e.g., C/C++, Python) are required. Expertise in algorithmics, protocol simulation, and network optimization will be positively valued. Fluent English level is mandatory. Speaking and writing French is optional but welcomed. We look for empathic, proactive, and self-driven applicants.

A propos d'Inria

Inria est l’institut national de recherche dédié aux sciences et technologies du numérique. Il emploie 2600 personnes. Ses 200 équipes-projets agiles, en général communes avec des partenaires académiques, impliquent plus de 3500 scientifiques pour relever les défis du numérique, souvent à l’interface d’autres disciplines. L’institut fait appel à de nombreux talents dans plus d’une quarantaine de métiers différents. 900 personnels d’appui à la recherche et à l’innovation contribuent à faire émerger et grandir des projets scientifiques ou entrepreneuriaux qui impactent le monde. Inria travaille avec de nombreuses entreprises et a accompagné la création de plus de 180 start-up. L'institut s'eﬀorce ainsi de répondre aux enjeux de la transformation numérique de la science, de la société et de l'économie.
