Context

The Inria FUN research group investigates solutions to enhance programmability, adaptability and reachability of FUN (Future Ubiquitous Networks) composed of wireless devices, Unmanned Aerial Vehicles (UAVs), connected vehicles. Limited resources, and high mobility evolving in distrusted environments characterize the objects that compose FUN. They communicate in a wireless way. To be operational and efficient, such networks have to follow some self-organizing rules. Indeed, components of FUN have to be able in a distributed and energy-efficient way to discover the network, self-deploy, communicate, self- structure in spite of their hardware constraints while adapting the environment in which adapting the environment in which they evolve. For additional information on the FUN research group, please see http://team.inria.fr/fun/

Assignment

Missions :

The postdoc will develop approaches for resource allocation and anomaly detection in the context of wireless communication systems, by considering different wireless communication technologies. The resource allocation mechansisms will explicitly consider the energy consumption and will minimize this parameter. The anomaly detection will be also based on energy profiles of the devices/users. Ad-hoc cyber attacks will be designed and implemented in order to characterize the resilience of the ssolutions proposed. The postodc will be in charge to develop pexperimental evaluation of the propsoed mechanisms, by considering heterogeneous wireless networks (i.e., edge-based architecture constotuted by connected objects with different capabilities and resources). 

Collaboration :

The recruited person will be supervised by Valeria Loscri (http://researchers.lille.inria.fr/~loscri/home.html) and Romain Rouvoy (https://romain.rouvoy.fr/).

The recruited person will be in charge to develop innovative solutions in the context of wireless communication, based on the interaction of different communication technologies in order to develop a robust and resilient to different attacks framework and advanced anomaly detection solutions. It will setup a set of experiments to prove the advanced robustness, resilience and real-time response, based on advanced AI/ML solutions.
