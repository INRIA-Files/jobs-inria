Context

The goal of this project is to estimate the probability that the error associated with a sensor on a vehicle exceeds a given threshold. For large threshold values, this problem can be reformulated in terms of estimating small tail probabilities, and conventional statistical methods are not applicable. As part of a preliminary study conducted in collaboration with a car equipment manufacturer, it has been shown that extreme-value theory can however be an adapted tool [1]

Assignment

The mission of the engineer is to deepen this study in several directions.

  • Validation of hypotheses. Implementation of extreme-value methods requires verification of a number of hypotheses: stationarity and temporal independence. On the first point, one can rely on segmented data according to homogeneous driving conditions. On the second point, independence tests will be carried out and, if necessary, an adaptation of the estimators to the dependent framework will be adopted.
  • Comparison of several approaches. Several estimation variants are available in the literature and will be compared: modeling of the two distribution tails simultaneously or separately, modeling of excesses or maxima, maximum likelihood estimationor by method of moments, restriction to a domain of attraction, ...
  • Efficient numerical implementation of the choser method.
  • Study of the robustness of the estimators. The quality of the estimates will be evaluated on the sample range (via cross-validation methods) and out of the sample range. In particular, we will study the extrapolation range depending on the size of the initial sample.

References:

[1] S. Girard ``Estimation of small tail probabilities - Application to the analysis of errors on sensor measurements'', Technical report, 2017.
[2] A. Asljung ``On Safety Validation of Automated Driving Systems using Extreme Value Theory'', Thesis for the Degree of Licentiate of Engineering, Chalmers University of Technology, 2017.
[3] D. Asljung, J.Nilsson & J. Fredriksson ``Comparing Collision Threat Measures for Verification of Autonomous Vehicles using Extreme Value Theory'', IFAC-PapersOnLine, 49-15, 057-062, 2016.
