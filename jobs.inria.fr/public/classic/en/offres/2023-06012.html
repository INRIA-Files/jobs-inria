Assignment

Within the framework of the ERC Advanced Grant G-Statistics (ending 08-2024), an engineer position of 3 month is offered on the Statistical Analysis of Population of Unlabelled Networks.

The analysis of population of networks is of high interest in both theory and applications. Network-valued data represent a challenging data type, for which very little statistical tools have been developed due the difficulties in using standard manifold statistics. Developing statistical methods for population of networks could find applications and solutions in different fields, from medicine to mobility, from economy to epidemiology. The objective of this project is to develop statistical tools for population of unlabelled networks seen as geometric objects with a quotient structure. Using the graph space embedding or more natural embedding spaces with a stratified structure, the project will focus on Principal Component Analysis, extending Barycentric Subspaces for population of unlabelled networks. The developed techniques as well as the general quotient space framework will be implemented within the geomstat python package.

Main activities

The objective of this 3 month period is to design dimensionality reduction techniques for the analysis of set of graphs. Given a set of labelled or unlabelled graphs, we want to implement a backward dimensionality reduction strategy to find a lower dimensional graph representation. The backward approach allows to interpret the dimensionality reduction and offer a representation capturing the graph variability. The candidate should be able to both develop theoretical methods and implementing the method in the software geomstats.

Skills

Applicants for this research engineering position must have a Ph.D thesis in mathematics or computer science related to geometry or statistics, with a publication record in high quality journals and conferences. They should justify an educational and/or work experience in both geometry and statistics, as well as in programming. A post-doc experience in statistics on graphs is required.
