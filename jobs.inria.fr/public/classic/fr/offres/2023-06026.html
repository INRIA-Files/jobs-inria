2023-06026 - PhD Position F/M Simulation of diffusions with singular coefficients

Fonction : Doctorant

Contexte et atouts du poste

Stochastic processes modeling the particle motion across interfaces which perturb the motion, have been object of intensive study in the last years. From the mathematical point of view the presence of interfaces of several kind introduces singularities. In the context of simulation, in the last decade, many methods have been developed in order to capture the singularities effects. Nevertheless, much has still to be done. More precisely, most of the methods are time-discretization-based approximations methods, and the very few exact simulation methods treat only the one-dimensional case. Most of the approximation methods do not capture the discontinuities concerning the diffusion coefficients or have not been proven to be convergent and how fast they converge. In general, exact simulation methods are difficult to obtain and they are computationally heavy. The aim of this thesis is to tackle this difficult problem of simulation of singular diffusions, by proposing a novel compromise between exact simulation and existing approximation methods.

Mission confiée

The goal of this thesis is to propose an epsilon-strong approximation method for a class of singular diffusions, called threshold diffusions. The first step is to study related existing methods namely the exact simulation methods [2,5], approximation methods [1], epsilon-strong approximation for diffusions with regular coefficients [3,5]. The second step is extrapolating the existing results to obtain new original ones, for threshold diffusions and in particular for solutions of stochastic differential equations with coefficients admitting jumps. The plan is to consider epsilon-strong approximation and a challenging mix with exact simulation. In this step the necessity of studying the transition densities of first hitting time could emerge. This means solving a partial differential equation, by using for instance some neural-networks techniques. The third step is the extension to higher dimension threshold processes for which results are rare. The proposed method can be naturally applied to

pricing of some local volatility models [4].

This project is connected through scientific cooperations with several European research institutions. Among them, we mention: University of Burgundy (France), Roma Tor Vergata (Rome).

A propos d'Inria

Inria est l’institut national de recherche dédié aux sciences et technologies du numérique. Il emploie 2600 personnes. Ses 200 équipes-projets agiles, en général communes avec des partenaires académiques, impliquent plus de 3500 scientifiques pour relever les défis du numérique, souvent à l’interface d’autres disciplines. L’institut fait appel à de nombreux talents dans plus d’une quarantaine de métiers différents. 900 personnels d’appui à la recherche et à l’innovation contribuent à faire émerger et grandir des projets scientifiques ou entrepreneuriaux qui impactent le monde. Inria travaille avec de nombreuses entreprises et a accompagné la création de plus de 180 start-up. L'institut s'eﬀorce ainsi de répondre aux enjeux de la transformation numérique de la science, de la société et de l'économie.
