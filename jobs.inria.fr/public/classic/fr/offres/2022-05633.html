Contexte et atouts du poste

Context. In the Ermine team at INRIA Rennes, we developed a technology for measuring and studying the main component of the Quality of Experience (QoE) of audio- and video-based applications and services on the Internet, the Perceptual Quality (PQ), the subjective way humans see the video or audio content. Quantifying this is hard because PQ is a subjective concept, lacking any possible formal definition. Our technology is called PSQA (Pseudo-Subjective Quality Assessment), it works automatically and in real-time if necessary, and it is as accurate as possible, replacing a panel of human observers. It is based on Supervised Learning (SL), and for this aspect of the construction of PSQA probes, we use a specific and not very well known family of tools called Random Neural Networks (RNNs).

 

Mission confiée

The work to be done consists of at least two parts, described in the sequel of this description.

Part 1. This first section of the work consists of writing a library implementing the tools necessary to use RNNs in SL. This needs to understand the mathematics behind RNNs, that comes from the stochastic processes area. The output will be divided into two modules, one for the particular case of feedforward RNNs (FF RNNs), the other one for the general case of recurrent architectures. Due to the nice mathematical properties of these objects, the library will offer also the tools for computing sensitivities of the networks with respect to the input variables (the partial derivatives of the output signals), in the two cases, FF RNNs and general ones.

Part 2. In the team, we are preparing a new version of PSQA called PSQA 2.0, where the tool will also predict the PQ of the application or service. For this purpose, we must be able to predict individual signals that can be measured objectively (not subjectively), such as the loss rate, the trafic intensity, etc. This makes necessary to implement algorithms able to do this prediction, known in the field as time series forecasting (t.s.p.). The task associated with this section includes the following components:
▪ to study some well known available methods for t.s.p., such as LGBM, TFT, prophet, LSTM variants, etc.;
▪ to implement an extension of RNNs developed in Ermine, called ESQN (Echo State Queueing Networks) which is a discrete time machine (RNNs work in continuous time when we see them as input-output dynamical systems);
▪ to implement another tool developed in our team, called EvoESN (Evolutionary Echo State Network)
▪ and to make comparisons of these methodologies on specific and well established benchmarks.
If time permits, several possible extensions of these works can be considered.

Compétences

Implementation constraints. The only constraint is to code in both python and C.

Necessary skills. For this work we need a candidate familiar with the maths associated with the project (combinatorics, probability, stochastic processes, queuing systems, scientific programming) plus with those of Machine Learning of course, and some experience with similar projects.
