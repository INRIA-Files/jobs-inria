Context

This Doctoral Research  position is proposed by the Mathrisk INRIA  Research team, https://team.inria.fr/mathrisk/en/, which is common with INRIA, the Ecole Nationale des Ponts et Chaussées and the University Gustave Eiffel. 

The  Mathrisk team addresses broad research topics in the area of  mathematical handling of risk, embracing risk measurement and risk management, modeling and optimization in quantitative finance and other related domains where risk control is paramount. It also develops a numerical platform for quantitative finance(http://www.premia.fr), supported by  a consortium of financial institutions. Mathematical expertise of the team include stochastic modeling, stochastic analysis, in particular stochastic (partial) differential equations and various aspects of stochastic control and optimal stopping of these equations, stochastic optimization, dynamic game theory, random graphs, martingale optimal transport and numerical probability.

 

Scientific context: 

The complexity of renewable energy systems, coupled with the pressing need to ensure their sustainable development and financial stability, calls for the exploration of advanced mathematical and computational techniques. This research project is motivated by the potential of Mean-Field Games (MFG), Graphon interaction, and stochastic control to address these challenges individually and collectively. Simultaneously, considerations of financial stability and systemic risk have become crucial for long-term sustainability, as they play a vital role in attracting investments and maintaining the resilience of energy markets.

The theory of Mean-field games, introduced by Lasry and Lions in~\cite{lasry2007mean} and Huang, Caines and Malham\'e \cite{huang2006large, huang2007large}, has raised a lot of attention these last years;   see in particular  the recent book \cite{carmona2018probabilistic} and references therein. MFG provides a powerful framework for modeling and analyzing the strategic interactions of a large number of agents within a given system. In the context of renewable energy systems, see e.g.~\cite{shrivats2022mean, carmona2022mean}, MFG can help capture the decision-making processes of agents as they invest in and manage renewable energy resources. 

 Graphons have been developed by Lov\'asz et al., see e.g.~\cite{lovasz2012, borgs2008convergent, borgs2012convergent},  as a natural continuum limit object for large dense graphs. Graphon interaction extends the capabilities of MFG by enabling the modeling of complex, large-scale networks of agents~\cite{caines2021graphon, amini2022graphon, bayraktar2020graphon}. This is particularly relevant in renewable energy systems, where agents' interactions can be highly dynamic and influenced by various factors, such as energy production, transmission, and consumption. By applying stochastic control and reinforcement learning to renewable energy systems, this research project aims to develop adaptive strategies that can respond to real-world uncertainties, enhancing the overall reliability and resilience of the energy system.

 

 

Assignment

The primary objective of this research project is to advance the understanding and application of Mean-Field Games (MFG) with graphon interaction and stochastic control in the context of renewable energy systems. By integrating these advanced mathematical and computational techniques, the project aims to create an innovative framework for modeling and optimizing renewable energy systems, while carefully considering financial stability and systemic risk. The research will focus on exploring the complex interactions between agents investing in renewable energy resources and their impact on system dynamics, overall energy production, and systemic risks.

The plan is to start with developing a strong background in renewable energy systems, their challenges, and the importance of financial stability and systemic risk. This will be followed by a comprehensive literature review focusing on Mean-Field Games (MFG), graphon interaction, and stochastic control in the context of energy systems. The study will then proceed with the development of a mathematical model incorporating MFG and graphon interaction to accurately represent the renewable energy system. Stochastic control techniques will then be applied to optimize system performance under various uncertainties. 

The proposed methodology will be tested on a real-world case study, with results evaluated against existing approaches. Sensitivity analysis will be performed to assess the robustness of the model and optimization strategies. The impact of agent interactions on system dynamics, energy production, and systemic risks will be analyzed in detail.
